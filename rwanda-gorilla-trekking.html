Volcanoes National Park, Rwanda

The World's Most Refined Gorilla Experience

Rwanda has reimagined what a private gorilla trek can be. In the shadow of the Virunga volcanoes, within a national park covering just 160 square kilometres of ancient Afromontane forest, a Rwanda gorilla trekking safari operates at a level of precision, comfort, and conservation rigor that distinguishes it entirely from any other wildlife encounter in Africa. This is luxury through restraint — a destination that protects its rarity by limiting and carefully managing every aspect of visitor access.

Volcanoes National Park is an easy three-hour drive from Kigali International Airport — one of Africa's most modern capitals. This proximity is unique: guests arrive from London, New York, or Paris and are in the forest within hours of landing, settled into some of the continent's most architecturally distinguished eco-lodges before dusk. The private gorilla trek Rwanda offers has no equivalent in terms of logistical elegance or accommodation calibre.

Each gorilla trekking permit in Rwanda allows a single hour with one habituated family, limited to eight visitors. The permit cost — among the highest for any wildlife experience on earth — funds ranger programmes, veterinary care, and the community tourism model that has made Rwanda's conservation approach a global template. When you trek with us, you witness this model in person, in a landscape where gorilla families are thriving and forests are expanding.

More Than a Safari

Conservation at the Core

Rwanda's approach to gorilla conservation is recognised globally as the most rigorous and effective model in existence. The country has built its entire tourism economy around the principle that the rarer an experience is, the more valuable — and the more protected — it must be. Every permit issued, every lodge built, every community programme funded traces directly back to this philosophy.

The controlled tourism numbers ensured by Rwanda's permit system mean your encounter involves no more than eight visitors, accompanied by trained rangers who have spent years habituating that specific family. This is not mass tourism. This is managed, science-led, community-funded conservation at its highest level.

Rwanda enforces the most rigorous gorilla trekking protocols in the world — mandatory health screening, minimum approach distances, and group size limits upheld without exception by trained rangers.

Community Tourism Impact

The Revenue Sharing Program routes a defined percentage of all park income directly to border communities — funding schools, health clinics, sustainable agriculture, and cultural preservation initiatives.

Controlled Tourism Numbers

A fixed number of permits are issued daily per gorilla family. This ceiling is set by conservation scientists and is never exceeded — protecting the families from stress, disease transmission, and behavioral disruption.
