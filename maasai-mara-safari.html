The Maasai Mara Experience

The World's Most Legendary Safari

A Maasai Mara safari is not simply a wildlife experience — it is an encounter with the raw, unfiltered drama of the natural world. Sprawling across 1,510 square kilometres of south-western Kenya, the Mara is home to the highest density of predators on earth and the setting for the annual Great Migration safari, one of the most staggering wildlife events ever witnessed. Between July and October, over two million wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle cross the crocodile-filled Mara River in thundering waves — a luxury Kenya safari moment that defies imagination.

Our private safari in Maasai Mara is designed for travelers who demand more than a standard game drive. Every detail — from your handpicked luxury tented camp to your expert guide's knowledge of each resident pride and cheetah coalition — is curated with precision. Big Five game drives take you off the main circuits into exclusive conservancies where vehicles are strictly limited and wildlife encounters are genuinely intimate.

Whether you select our 3-day itinerary for an immersive first encounter or the 5-day journey for a comprehensive East Africa safari experience, every game drive departs at your pace, every meal is an occasion, and every moment in the field is guided by one of our senior wildlife specialists.

Day-by-Day

Safari Itineraries

Both itineraries are fully private and customizable. Each day can be adapted to your pace, interests, and travel style.

1 Arrival

Arrival & Afternoon Game Drive

Transfer from Nairobi or fly-in to the Mara airstrip. Your guide meets you on arrival and briefs you on the days ahead over lunch at camp. The afternoon game drive launches into the Reserve at the golden hour — the most active period for predators. Settle into your tented suite as the sun sets over the savannah.

Flight or road transfer from Nairobi to Maasai Mara
Welcome lunch & camp orientation
Afternoon Big Five game drive (3–6pm)
Sundowners & dinner at camp
2 Full Day

Full Day Big Five Safari

A full day in the field — beginning before sunrise to track lions returning from a night hunt, through the midday hours following elephant herds and leopard, to the afternoon when the great cats become active again. Between July and October, the day may include witnessing a wildebeest river crossing at the Mara River — one of the most dramatic sights in the natural world.

Predawn departure for sunrise game drive
Bush breakfast in the field
Mara River crossing observation (seasonal, July–Oct)
Afternoon predator tracking & Big Five pursuit
3 Departure

Morning Game Drive & Departure

One final early morning drive through your favourite circuit before a late breakfast at camp. Luggage is loaded as you linger over coffee and your guide shares highlights from the past days. Transfer to the airstrip or road for your onward journey.

Early morning game drive (6–9am)
Final breakfast & farewell at camp
Transfer to Mara airstrip or Nairobi
1 Arrival

Arrival & Introduction to the Mara

Arrival at Mara airstrip or road transfer from Nairobi. Settle into your luxury tented camp and meet your personal guide over lunch. An introductory afternoon drive orients you to the landscape, resident prides, and the movement of the herds before a private dinner under the stars.

Fly-in or road transfer from Nairobi
Camp introduction & guide briefing
Orientation afternoon game drive
Private dinner at camp
2 Wildlife

Big Five Tracking

A dedicated day focused on locating and spending quality time with all five: lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino. Your guide uses radio communication with fellow trackers across the Reserve to position you for optimal wildlife encounters throughout the day.

Full-day Big Five tracking circuit
Bush lunch in the field
Leopard & cheetah territory focus
Sundowners at a scenic viewpoint
3 Signature

Balloon Safari & Bush Breakfast

The defining experience of any Maasai Mara safari — a pre-dawn transfer to the launch site for a one-hour flight over the awakening plains. The Mara at first light, from altitude, is a sight of extraordinary beauty. A champagne breakfast beneath an acacia tree follows before an afternoon drive focused on the Migration routes and river crossings.

Pre-dawn balloon launch over the Mara
One-hour flight over the savannah
Champagne bush breakfast
Afternoon migration & river crossing drive

Cultural Experience & Photography Safari

A morning visit to an authentic Maasai village offers rare insight into the community whose ancestral lands encompass the Reserve. Afternoons are dedicated to a slower, photography-focused game drive with your guide positioning the vehicle for optimal light and framing.

Maasai village visit with local guide
Cultural exchange & traditional ceremony

Your last morning in the Mara. An early drive to say farewell to the plains — often producing the finest sightings of the entire trip as the predators move in the cool light. A final breakfast at camp, then transfer to the airstrip or onward to your next destination.

Final morning game drive (6–9am)
Farewell breakfast at camp

Best Time to Visit the Mara

The Maasai Mara is a year-round destination, but each season offers a distinct and extraordinary experience. Our specialists will advise on the optimal timing for your specific interests.

Ask a Specialist
July — October
Migration Peak

The Great Migration wildebeest river crossings reach peak intensity. The most dramatic and iconic Maasai Mara experience. High season — book well in advance.

December — March
Predator Season

Dry conditions concentrate game around water sources. Outstanding lion, cheetah, and leopard sightings. Calving season draws large predator gatherings.

April — May
Green Season

The Mara transforms to vivid emerald. Excellent birdwatching, lush landscapes, and significantly reduced visitor numbers. Ideal for photography.
